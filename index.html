Welcome to the Astrogeology & Surface Simulation Lab (ASSL)

The Astrogeology and Surface Simulation Laboratory (ASSL), led by Dr. Trishit Ruj at the Institute for Planetary Materials, Okayama University, focuses on the exploration and analysis of planetary surfaces, particularly Mars, to unravel their geological history, environmental conditions, and potential habitability.

At ASSL, we employ cutting-edge interdisciplinary methods, integrating planetary geology, remote sensing, geomorphology, and experimental simulations. Our laboratory is equipped with custom-built Martian environmental simulation chambers, enabling precise replication of planetary surface processes to better interpret remote observations and analog studies.

We are dedicated to advancing planetary science by bridging laboratory experiments, field analog research, and computational modeling. Our international collaborations span Japan, India, Europe, and beyond, contributing valuable insights to ongoing and future planetary exploration missions.

Our team is committed to fostering an inclusive and dynamic research environment, encouraging innovative approaches, and preparing the next generation of scientists to tackle key questions about planetary evolution, climate dynamics, and astrobiology.
