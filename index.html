    About

    The Astrogeology and Surface Simulation Laboratory (ASSL), led by Dr. Trishit Ruj at the Institute for Planetary Materials, Okayama University, is dedicated to investigating the surface evolution and habitability of planetary bodies—particularly Mars.

    Our interdisciplinary research bridges planetary geology, geomorphology, remote sensing, machine learning, and analog experiments. We recreate Martian surface conditions using custom-built environmental simulation chambers, analyze terrain using satellite data, and interpret landform histories through both lab-based and field analog studies.

    With active collaborations across Japan, India, and Europe, the lab contributes to mission planning, data interpretation, and the broader understanding of planetary surface processes and their implications for past water activity and life.

    Research

    Our work spans Mars analog studies, planetary geomorphology, environmental chamber experiments, and AI-assisted surface classification. We aim to better understand the geological history and potential habitability of Mars and other planetary bodies.
